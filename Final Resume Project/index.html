A Little Bit About Myself

I am a journalism student studying at the Philip Merrill College of Journalism at the University of Maryland. After graduating high school in Columbus, Ohio, I moved to Maryland to pursue a career in journalism. I caught the news bug after being in the D.C. area for only a few weeks. My freshman roommate and I huddled in our dorm and watched Christine Blasey Ford give her testimony about Brett Kavanaugh. My roommate had a Spanish quiz to study for and I had a book report to write but we sat mesmerized in front of my laptop with popcorn and Skittles. This was happening in our backyard. We felt so close to the news that we could taste it. I knew in that moment I was hooked.

I started writing for the Writer’s Bloc, University of Maryland’s primary publication for the arts, during my first semester of college. My first college all-nighter was spent writing an impassioned review of Netflix’s newest release only to discover I had no idea how to write anything other than a high school essay. After taking a deep dive into my journalism classes and picking up every pitch I could get my hands on, I found my footing. During my first two years of college I worked as a staff writer for two campus publications, the Writer’s Bloc and the Campus Trainer, a student-run lifestyle publication. Now that I am a section editor at the Campus Trainer, I spend my all-nighters forming pitches and editing the articles of freshman who are just starting out. as a student in the Philip Merrill College of Journalism I have learned invaluable skills in both print and online journalism. My work as a student journalist has included gathering, writing and presenting stories that clearly and stylishly communicate important and interesting information to readers in the community.

Outside of being a student I have a passion for reading, theatre and scuba diving. In 2017, I recieved my open-ocean scuba diving certification and plan on taking many underwater journeys in the future.
